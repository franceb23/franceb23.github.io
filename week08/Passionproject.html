In this space I will explain how I saw sneaker reselling as a way to be financially savy and understand some of the many concepts of financial literacy, and how it changed my views on consumerism and give you a glimpse of my shoe collection and there value and some of shoes that are holy grails of mine.

How I started

I started my passion of sneakers first by seeing youtube video's about the latest jordan drop and that made me curious about Michael Jordan himself and what made him the greatest of all time in basketball and of sneaker culture for 20 plus years. So this curiosity made me purchase my first pair of jordans which turned out to be a replica pair of air jordan 14 black toe. I should have known they were replica's being only $40, but like in anything in life you live and you learn and I didnt allow this to discourage me from buying more of which I did and started buying shoes which were a bit cheaper than jordans but once had a hold of the sneaker culture which where nike sb's which became my actuall gate way to sneaker culture and had me foaming at the mouth at thousand dollar shoes wishing and hoping one day I will be able to buy such a coveted piece of sneaker history. So in my journey I started buying shoes that at the time were only going for $100 retail and now in todays resell market go for $350 plus and all the shoes I bought at the time were shoes that held so musch significance to me like going on my first date with a pair of nike sb high 'black box', my first car accident in my nike sb 'snake eyes' and many other stories with similar ups and downs that life brings. I started reselling sneakers first by reselling a popular streetwear brand named supreme that at the time had a huge return on investment of like 80% in which at that time I was a broke college student in North Lake College getting my associates in business administration so I had a couple classes that tought me a couple things about investing and finacial literacy and never knew that something like clothes and sneakers can have a huge return on investment. So it got me very excited that two things like sneaker's and finances had a huge parallel because they were two new found passion's of mine that worked amazing with one another.

Sneaker's = Finance?

For me I never started collecting shoes for the money I can potentially make on them but because it was something that I really wanted to understand more about and also I always had a knack of collecting things like Yugioh cards, marbels, and figurines. But one day after I had my accident and needed money I soon found out the importance of a nest egg and to always have a saving's in place in case of a emergency and at that time my nest egg were my shoes which unfortunately meant that I had to sell a bunch of them and let go of the memories I had with them which was somber at the time but I knew that the situation in front of me had more of an importance and that thing's come and go. So when I ended up selling a bunch of my shoes I had goal in mind that when I got back on feet financially I would love to rebuild that collection back up again. So another thing I did was to split my money into envelopes for things like bills, savings, and shoe's. which ended up being a great idea and I was finally able to buy some shoes I either had or wanted which really made me feel happy that simple things like that helped me reach goals that I thought were only possible with a bigger paycheck and all it took was patience and being discplined enough to buy things only when I needed to or if I know I can make money on it due to trends I see while reselling on the side and nothing more because I didnt want to break the promises I made to myself. So I was really on the Grind for a couple years saving what little I could to buy shoes and also being able to save up for a car and a rainy day fund for any future emergency. In that time I also started to get more familiar with common investment strategies like stock's and indexes which I try to fund on a continuos basis, which ultimately hits on the topic of wealth preservation and the ability to understand that investment's in any form can preserve wealth if bought in small chunks over a long period of time and till this day has helped me very graciously so far there is a quote from Charlie Munger that says "time in the market always beats timing the market" which I believe in any market that has a supply and demand, I buy whether that be stock's, crypto, or shoe's all of which are dictated by supply and demand of said assets, atleast to me that means they have a market share and all of which I consider to be investment's of mine.
